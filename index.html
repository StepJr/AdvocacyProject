The Overview

According to the CDC, fentanyl is a synthetic opioid that is up to 50 times stronger than heroin and 100 times stronger than morphine. It is a major contributor to fatal and nonfatal overdoses in the U.S.

The Impact

According to the National Institutes of Health,
when fentanyl is prescribed by a doctor, there aren't many deaths.
Many people are dying from fentanyl because they are getting it from illict sources.
These illegal fentanyl are made in labs and are sold as a powder, dropped
onto blotter paper, put in eye droppers and nasal sprays, or made into pills that
look like other prescription opioids. But what's even more dangerous is that some
drug dealers are mixing fentanyl with other drugs, such as heroin, cocaine,
methamphetamine, and MDMA. This is risky becasue the people who are taking
those drugs don't realize that they might contain fentanyl. Their body may not be
used to how strong fentanyl is and this could easily lead to a overdose. That
exactly how "Cruz," the boy in the video above sadly died. He though he was
simply taking percocet but it was laced with fentanyl.
